Edge Jumps

What are edge jumps?

Loop jump is the skater takes off from his back outside edge and lands on the back outside edge of the same leg. An easy way to remember this jump is that it's basically a toe loop without the assist of the toe pick.

The Salchow starts from the back inside edge (if you were standing straight up, the edges that face each other) and lands on the back outside edge of the opposite foot.

The Axel is an edge jump, which means that the skater must spring into the air from the outside edge. The Axel is the easiest jump to tell apart from the other because of its entry -- it is the only one has the forward takeoff. It is the oldest but most difficult figure skating jump
